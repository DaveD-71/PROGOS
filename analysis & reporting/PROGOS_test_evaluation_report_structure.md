# PROGOS Test Evaluation Report Structure

## Independent Evaluation of the PROGOS English Speaking Test

1. **Executive Summary**

2. **Purpose and Intended Use**
   - Stated and inferred purposes
   - Appropriate and inappropriate uses

3. **Test Methodology**
   - Test overview and administration
   - Task design
   - Evaluation of Parts 1–5
   - Item-level strengths and weaknesses

4. **Validity**
   - Construct validity
   - Content validity
   - Authenticity
   - Interpretive validity
   - Consequential validity
   - Interaction and reading-aloud limitations
   - Claims made in score reports

5. **Reliability**
   - Administrative reliability
   - Scoring reliability
   - Limitations of the available evidence

6. **Test Instructions and Language**
   - Language and clarity of instructions
   - Cognitive and listening demands
   - Accessibility
   - Construct-irrelevant variance

7. **Fairness and Accessibility**
   - Topic familiarity
   - Workplace, age, and cultural bias
   - Accommodations

8. **Practicality**
   - Cost and scalability
   - Administration
   - Reporting speed
   - Ease of use

9. **Evaluation of the Score Reports**
   - Report structure
   - Analytic scores
   - Overall assessment comments
   - Diagnostic usefulness
   - Individualisation and evidence of templating
   - Transparency of score interpretation

10. **Legal and Ethical Considerations**
    - Privacy and terms of use
    - AI governance
    - Voice recordings
    - Transparency

11. **Suitability for the Organisation's Purposes**
    - Class allocation
    - Pre/post testing
    - Course design
    - Teacher selection

12. **Conclusions and Recommendations**
    - Summary of findings
    - Limitations
    - Use-case recommendations

| Use case | Recommended status |
|---|---|
| Broad benchmarking | Supplementary only |
| Placement | Supplementary only |
| Pre/post testing | Not recommended |
| Course design | Not recommended |
| Teacher assignment | Not recommended |
| Diagnostic assessment | Not recommended |
